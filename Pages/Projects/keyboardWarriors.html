Project Requirements

Our general task was to find a group and to separate into three roles; Product Owner, Scrum Master, and the Development Team. I was a part of the Development Team along with two others, meaning we were in charge of nearly everything involving our application. Our main task was creating any playable application, but we also had to keep track of our progress, which we did via GitHub and Monday.


Project Vision

I was directly involved with choosing our project idea, where I took inspiration from Type to Learn, which I was introduced to during Elementary and Middle School.

Akin to Type to Learn, we wanted to improve our user's ability to type efficiently, although the former was for computer keyboards. Thus, we shifted our vision to the crucial skill that is typing on your phone.


Project Description

Keyboard Warriors contains four main levels; a Tutorial, and Easy, Medium, and Hard Mode. The user's goal is to defeat all 25 enemies in a level (aside from the tutorial, which holds different rules) by typing out the displayed words correctly. Each difficulty has their own quirks, with Easy having 3-4 letter words and 10 seconds to type, Medium with 7, and Hard with 5 seconds and more complex words. If the user incorrectly submits a word, or runs out of time, three separate times, the game is over. Players start with 3 hearts, and each time a blemish occurs, they lose one.

Normal enemies can be taken out with one correct word, but after 25 have been defeated, the user is transported to the Boss Battle. The Boss has it's own unique sprite and requires 5, 7, and 10 correct words, respectively, to defeat it.
